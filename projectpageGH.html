GrubHub, 2012-2014

When I joined GrubHub, it was still a start-up, and many of my projects were broad and undefined. This was a great environment to do exploratory research, since there was not yet a large body of research, as well as to create and test mockups iteratively, based on usability tests. My work was often self-directed.

As we grew and merged with a competitor, I was very focused on creatively communicating research results: based on time and geographic constraints, I presented research in a variety of formats to diverse groups of stakeholders in different time zones (executives, product managers, engineers, fellow user experience professionals).

As a start-up, we practiced some Agile methodologies, specifically iterative design and daily stand-up meetings.

Prototyping Tools

The goal of the project determined the prototyping tools I used.

Some projects were exploratory, best served using "guerilla research" techniques and very low-fidelity prototypes, while others were longer term, requiring higher-fidelity prototypes and more extensive research planning.

A few of the tools I used at GrubHub: Adobe Illustrator, Fireworks, Photoshop, Omnigraffle, HTML, CSS. I've also used Axure and iRise to create mock-ups for non-GrubHub projects.

User Research

I used many types of user research methods. While interviews and usability tests were often most appropriate, I also performed contextual inquires, created surveys, diary studies, card sorting sessions, and master/apprentice observations.

Occasionally non-user research was most appropriate, such as a competitive analysis or simply reviewing and collating research that had already been performed.

Company Change

After my first year at GrubHub, we merged with our closest competitor, which was based in New York.

I realized I had a unique position in the company since I had done interviews with both “brands” of our customers after our merger. As a side project, I began integrating research from diverse projects in order to compare and contrast the user views in these two major markets. This became especially important as we sought to integrate our two product teams.
